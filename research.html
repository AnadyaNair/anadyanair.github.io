Analysis of Acid Rain: A Global Perspective

Published September 2023 | Read Paper |

Acid rain is a major environmental issue that is caused by human activities such as burning fossil fuels and has far-reaching impacts on ecosystems, including pollution levels, geographical and ornithological effects, and the potential to disrupt the food chain. To mitigate these impacts, it is necessary to understand the causes of acid rain, the chemistry of precipitating acids, how to identify it, and its historical and mythological mentions. Public perception, including the opinions of common people, farmers, artisans, and scientists, plays an important role in creating awareness and implementing preventive measures. Images of acid rain impacts serve as a reminder of the devastating effects of human activities on the environment. Without adequate preventive measures, acid rain could worsen and lead to further environmental degradation, highlighting the urgent need for governments and individuals to take action and implement sustainable practices to prevent further damage.
